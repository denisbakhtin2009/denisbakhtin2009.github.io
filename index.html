History of creation

Xiaomi was founded by eight partners on April 6, 2010. On August 16, 2010, Xiaomi officially launched the MIUI firmware, based on the Android system and combining the styles of Samsung TouchWiz and Apple iOS. The first smartphone - Mi 1 was announced in August 2011. It came in two versions - with MIUI firmware and with ‚Äúclean‚Äù Android. In August 2012, the company introduced the Mi 2 smartphone; by September 2013, more than 10 million devices were sold. On September 5, 2013, CEO Lei Jun announced plans to launch a 47-inch Android 3D TV, which will be assembled at the Wistron factory in Taiwan. In September 2013, Xiaomi announced the Mi 3 smartphone. On September 25, 2013, plans were announced to open the first flagship store in Beijing. In October 2013, Xiaomi became the fifth mobile brand in China. The symbol of Xiaomi is a hare in an earflaps with a red star and a red pioneer tie around his neck. In 2014, Xiaomi first announced its international expansion in connection with the opening of its first store in Singapore. The company announced plans to enter the markets of Malaysia and India. On March 17, 2014, Redmi Note (also known as HongMi Note) was announced. Redmi Note was equipped with a 5.5-inch HD IPS display with OGS technology and an eight-core processor from MediaTek. In April 2014, the company announced the beginning of expansion into international markets of ten countries, including Russia. One of the former Google employees Hugo Barra was appointed responsible. In June 2015, Xiaomi signed a contract with the Russian company Marvel-Distribution for the supply of MiPad tablets. On June 18, 2015, Xiaomi MiPad tablets officially began to be sold in the Svyaznoy retail chain. At the end of 2016, the company did not get into the top ten in terms of the number of devices sold and the revenue from their sale.
